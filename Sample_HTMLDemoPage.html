Accessibility Course

WCAG

It is a set of international standards that help make websites and apps accessible to everyone — especially people with disabilities (like vision, hearing, motor, or cognitive issues).

WCAG Principles

WCAG Principles are the 4 main rules that guide how to make websites accessible to people with disabilities. These principles are known as POUR:

  1. Perceivable
  2. Operable
  3. Understandable
  4. Robust

Perceivable

Perceivable means that all users — including those with visual or hearing disabilities — must be able to see, hear, or feel the content.

  • Text Alternatives
  • Time-Based Media
  • Adaptable Content
  • Distinguishable
Alternative Text Guidelines

Provide text descriptions for all non-text content like images, audio, video, buttons, icons, and charts — so that screen readers or other assistive tools can explain them.

Non-text Content

1.1.1 Non-text Content
All non-text content must have a text alternative that serves the same purpose.
